THE RATIONALE
BRIEF
Art Gallery of N.S.W is one of Australia’s leading museum. However, the user experience does not match the experience and inspiration of the gallery itself. Our challenge is to understand their needs from the complex audience groups, and then integrate this understanding into their customer experience.

RESEARCH FINDINGS
Many people love art and want to learn more about it. They also want to bring their experience back and be able to share it. Individual's often try to collect information by taking photos of the art piece or information plaques on their mobile. Unfortunately, in most cases there was not enough detail on each art piece that they could gain a deeper understanding from. A large proportion of the sample where either students learning art or individuals who were art novelist.

THE PROBLEM
The main obstacle was the lack of information in the art gallery that could help the students and art novelist learn more about each art piece. The difficulty in the current plaques in the art gallery is that they lack in depth information, and in order to view the plaques individuals are required to gather around the plaque and thereby block the visibility of the art piece. Furthermore, individuals found it difficult to retain or remember each art piece they saw.

SOLUTION
Create a collective experience that captures what they see at the art gallery and gives them the ability to store the experience in a way that they can either review the information straight away or later. Once they take the photo of the art piece, Google's image search engine will find the art piece and populate the app with content from the art gallery archive. If the art gallery doesn't hold enough information, you have the option to search Google for more. The app will have the capability of giving in depth content to enrich each art piece with additional audio, video, images, to compliment and enhance the experience.
